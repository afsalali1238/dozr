01
What dozr is

dozr is a B2B marketplace for construction logistics in the UAE. You need a flatbed trailer on a jobsite in Al Quoz by 7am — dozr finds it, books it, tracks it, and proves it arrived. You need a 320 excavator for three weeks in Abu Dhabi — dozr puts it there with a GPS signal and a utilisation report that becomes your invoice.

dozr operates at the intersection of two industries that have not been digitised: heavy equipment rental and construction freight. Both run on phone calls, WhatsApp messages, paper logbooks, and relationships. dozr replaces the friction without replacing the relationships.

The one-sentence brand truth: dozr puts the right machine on the right site and proves it worked.
What it is

A marketplace with a data layer

Book equipment and freight from a vetted UAE network. Every job generates GPS data, fuel records, engine hours, and driver timesheets. The data is the product.

What it is not

Not a SaaS dashboard for enterprises

Not Samsara. Not a fleet management tool for a logistics company's own fleet. dozr serves the operator renting out their excavator and the contractor hiring it — both sides.

02
The market

The UAE construction sector is spending AED 350B+ on active projects across Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and the northern emirates. Every one of those projects needs equipment and materials moved. None of that movement is currently managed through a single digital platform.

Primary segment

Construction logistics

Flatbed trailers moving building materials, steel, and prefab between ports, factories, and sites. Khor Fakkan, Fujairah, Jebel Ali, Al Quoz, KIZAD — these are the routes that build the UAE.

Secondary segment

Equipment rental

Excavators, cranes, loaders, dump trucks, generators rented by the day, week, or month to construction projects. Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and the northern emirates are the core market.

The market currently operates through informal networks — a site manager calls three people, gets three different prices, and goes with whoever can confirm fastest. There is no transparency on rates, no visibility on equipment condition, and no proof of delivery or utilisation for billing disputes.

The problem dozr solves in one sentence: Nobody knows where the excavator is, how long it ran, or whether the delivery actually happened — until dozr.

The audience

Three distinct users. Each sees a different interface. Each has a different relationship with the dozr brand. The design system must work across all three without losing coherence.

🏗️
The Client
Site manager · Procurement · Project director
  • Needs equipment or freight, fast
  • Wants price certainty
  • Needs proof it happened (billing, disputes)
  • Expects WhatsApp-speed communication
  • Does not want to manage a fleet
🚛
The Vendor
Fleet owner · Equipment operator · Independent contractor
  • Owns 3–30 trucks or machines
  • Manages drivers via WhatsApp
  • Cannot afford enterprise fleet software
  • Wants to reduce idle time and fuel theft
  • Needs billing proof to get paid faster
📱
The Driver
Equipment operator · Truck driver · On-site staff
  • Uses the platform on a phone, not a desktop
  • Non-technical — interface must be obvious
  • Receives WhatsApp alerts, not email
  • May not be fluent in English
  • Works in dusty, high-heat conditions
Design implication: Every screen must work at arm's length, in sunlight, with a gloved hand on a ruggedized phone. This is not a product for a boardroom. Minimum tap targets 48px. High contrast. Plain labels. One action per screen.

Brand personality

If dozr were a person, they are a site foreman who has been on every job in Dubai. They know the cost of a breakdown. They know that a truck that didn't show up means a crane is idle. They do not use jargon. They do not over-promise. They get things done and they can prove it.

DirectNo fluff. Says the number.
ReliableDoes what it says.
TransparentShows the data.
ProvenHas the receipts.

Honest machines

Show the rate, the tonnage, the engine hours, the fuel consumption. Real numbers build trust faster than any claim. If a machine has 4,182 engine hours on it, say so.

On site by 7am

Construction starts at first light. dozr's value is speed and certainty. Everything — copy, UI, response times — should feel like it respects that urgency without manufacturing fake urgency.

Yellow means action

One action per screen. Yellow is reserved for the thing that needs to happen next. If everything is highlighted, nothing is. Restraint is a brand decision.

No paperwork

The GPS data is the logbook. The ePOD is the delivery note. The engine hours report is the invoice. dozr eliminates the paper, not just digitises it.

09
Competitor context

Understanding how others look and sound helps define where dozr should not go. The category is split between enterprise-grade platforms (polished, cold, complex) and informal alternatives (WhatsApp, no platform at all). dozr lives between them — the intelligence of enterprise, the speed of WhatsApp.

Enterprise GPS platforms — avoid their look

Samsara, Geotab, Fleetio

Navy blue, corporate grey, dense dashboards, enterprise sales motion, 3-year contracts. They feel like accounting software for a logistics multinational. dozr's vendors cannot afford or navigate this. The brand should feel lighter and faster.

Local alternatives — occupy the gap they leave

WhatsApp, Excel, phone calls

The current reality for most UAE construction logistics. No brand. No interface. No data. dozr replaces this without requiring the user to become technical. The brand should feel accessible to someone who currently manages everything on WhatsApp.

The visual brief in one line: dozr should look like it was built for a site manager, not designed for a VC pitch deck — but finished with enough precision that it earns trust at first glance.
Spatial references that work for dozr

Jobsite aesthetics

Safety yellow on near-black. High contrast for sunlight readability. Geometric and grid-based. Heavy type. Clean and undecorated. Information-dense but not cluttered. The visual language of a blueprint or a spec sheet — not a startup landing page.

Aesthetics to avoid

Generic SaaS / logistics clichés

Gradient hero backgrounds. Abstract "speed lines." Stock photos of trucks on highways. Blue-and-green "trust" palettes. Rounded, friendly, consumer-app aesthetics. Animated dashboards with no real data. These signal "tech startup," not "construction operations."

Design implications

The brand context above is not abstract. Each point has a direct design consequence. These are the decisions the brand guidelines need to make — and the context behind each one.

Design decisionContext behind it
Yellow as the single action colourSafety yellow is the only colour the site manager already trusts. It means something is happening or needs to happen. Overuse kills the signal.
Ink (near-black) as the dominant fieldConstruction equipment is heavy, dark, industrial. The brand should carry that weight without feeling dark or intimidating. Ink lets yellow and white read cleanly.
Space Mono for data and specsEngine hours, fuel levels, prices, GPS coordinates — these are measurements. Monospace type communicates precision. It is the visual language of a spec sheet.
No shadows, no gradients on cardsSite managers see blurred lines in sunlight. Clean flat borders with strong contrast survive any screen condition. Shadows are decoration — remove them.
48px minimum tap targetsGloved hands. Ruggedized tablets. Bright sunlight. The interface must work in conditions where most apps break.
Photography: real machines, real sitesDust, wear, real conditions. Never staged, never studio-lit, never a stock photo of a truck on a highway. Real machines back up "honest numbers."
One CTA per screenThe user wants to book, track, or report. Not explore. Not discover. Every screen has one job and one yellow button.
WhatsApp-native alert designAlerts must be readable as plain text in a WhatsApp message. No rich formatting. No embedded links that don't work on mobile. The message itself must carry the information.
Arabic script considerationA significant portion of drivers and vendors are more comfortable in Arabic. The type system must accommodate RTL without breaking the layout or losing the brand's precision.
Two-product coherencedozr Marketplace and dozr Fleet are different surfaces for different users — but one brand. The Marketplace should feel like the entry point. Fleet should feel like the deeper instrument panel of the same machine.
The design brief in two sentences: Build something a site manager trusts the first time they see it. Build something a fleet owner uses every morning without thinking about the brand — because it just works.
