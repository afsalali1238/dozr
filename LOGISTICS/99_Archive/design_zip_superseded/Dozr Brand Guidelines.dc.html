Audience

Three users, three interfaces, one brand. The system has to hold across all of them without losing coherence.

Site manager · procurement
Needs equipment or freight, fast · wants price certainty · needs proof it happened · expects WhatsApp-speed replies

Fleet owner · operator
Owns 3–30 machines · runs drivers on WhatsApp · can't afford enterprise software · wants less idle time and fuel theft
The Driver
Operator · on-site staff
On a phone, not a desktop · non-technical · gets WhatsApp alerts · may prefer Arabic · works in dust and heat
Design consequence
Every screen must work at arm's length, in sunlight, with a gloved hand on a ruggedized phone. 48px minimum tap targets. High contrast. Plain labels. One action per screen. Arabic / RTL must not break the layout.

Part two — System
The visual & verbal toolkit
01
Principles

Built for the site, not the boardroom.

Dozr is used by contractors, site managers and equipment buyers — people who want the machine, the price and the date, fast. Every screen earns its keep by being obvious.

01  Minimal & white
White space is the default. Nothing decorative. If it doesn't help someone rent a machine, it goes.
02  Self-explanatory
Plain labels, big touch targets, one clear action per screen. No manual required.
03  Yellow means go
Yellow appears only on the action that matters. If everything is yellow, nothing is.
04  Real numbers, real machines
Show the rate, the tonnage, the photo. Specs build trust faster than adjectives.

03
Photography

Equipment is shot in-situ — real jobsites, real conditions, dust and wear left visible. No studio cleanup, no cutouts on white. Honest machines back up “real numbers, real machines.”

Crop
4:3 ratio
Consistent across cards and detail pages.
Framing
Machine fills 70–80%
Fill the frame — no tiny machine lost in landscape.
Light
Natural daylight only
No artificial studio lighting or rim lights.
Treatment
No cutout-on-white
Keep the ground, the site, the context.
● Use IN-SITU · DAYLIGHT · 4:3
On site, dust and wear visible, machine filling the frame.
✕ Avoid STAGED · CUTOUT ON WHITE
Studio-lit, isolated, stock-photo perfect. Reads fake.
Fallback
If a supplier can't provide an acceptable photo, use the branded placeholder (Ink background, blade mark centered, no stock imagery) — never a stretched or blurry vendor photo.

Language rules
Use AED, not “cost-effective”
Real numbers build trust. Vague value claims don't.
Sentence case everywhere
Title Case reads corporate.
Active verbs
“Book,” not “Get a quote for.”
No exclamation marks
A site manager doesn't exclaim. Neither does dozr.
Specificity over superlatives
“2 hours to confirm,” not “fast.”
Machine names & model numbers
“CAT 320,” not “heavy excavator.”
Short sentences. Real numbers. Talk like someone standing on the site, not sitting in a boardroom.
