The Problem — What's happening right now without Kasper
📞
5–10 phone calls per job
A project manager needs one truck from Khor Fakkan to Dubai. He calls 6 brokers. 4 don't answer. 2 quote over the phone with nothing in writing.
📍
Zero tracking
"I'll call the driver" is the standard response when a client asks where the truck is. There is no live visibility at any point in the job.
📄
Paper PODs go missing
Delivery proof is a paper note in a driver's cab. It gets wet, torn, or lost. Disputes take weeks. Invoices get blocked with no proof of delivery to attach.
🗓️
7–14 day invoicing
Invoice arrives long after the job. The client has moved on. Cash flow is destroyed on both sides because nobody can close the job loop on time.
💬
No audit trail
Pricing disputes, damage claims, timing disagreements — nothing is provable. The entire job lifecycle lives in someone's WhatsApp and memory.
🔁
Ops bottleneck at every step
Every update requires a human call or message. The ops team can only handle 8–10 simultaneous jobs without things falling through the cracks.
How We Solve It — Build sequence: Client+Ops first, then Vendor, then Driver
Why this sequence
We are already running jobs manually for real clients. Step 1 is to make the client booking and ops coordination digital. We can still use WhatsApp with vendors temporarily. Once the client–ops flow is solid and we have real job data, we add the vendor portal so vendors self-serve. Then we add the driver app for GPS and ePOD. Each step removes one manual bottleneck.

PRODUCT 02
Kasper Vendor OS
● Building Now — Phase 2
Once the marketplace is running and we have real vendor relationships, we package the same tools we built for ourselves into a standalone SaaS product. A 1-truck operator can get a booking link, quote in 60 seconds, send a VAT-compliant invoice, and track their driver — all from WhatsApp. Starts at AED 299/month. Less than AED 10/day.
WhatsApp-native 60-sec quoting PDF invoice auto Live GPS Digital ePOD AED 299–999/month
The Problem — UAE logistics SME reality
📝
Quoting by voice note
Most 1–10 truck operators receive a WhatsApp voice note from a client asking for a price. They reply with another voice note. Nothing is written. Nothing is legally binding. Disputes are guaranteed.
🧾
Invoices from Word or photos
The invoice is a Word document template with the price typed in, or a photo of a handwritten note. No TRN. No VAT breakdown. Clients with legal departments won't pay these. Jobs get held up for weeks.
📲
Tracking by phone call
"Where's my truck?" is answered by calling the driver. The vendor then calls the client back. This loop happens 3–5 times per job. Every call costs time and destroys the professionalism of the service.
💰
No idea if they made money
Small operators have no dashboard. At the end of the month they count bank deposits and receipts. They have no idea which driver was profitable, which client pays on time, or what their margin per job is.
🗂️
Enterprise clients won't use them
Large construction companies and developers want proper documentation: VAT-compliant invoice, delivery note with signature, real tracking. Small operators cannot provide this, so they lose those contracts.
⚠️
Can't scale beyond 2–3 trucks
Without digital tools, the owner has to personally manage every job. When they add a second or third truck, coordination becomes impossible. Growth is capped by the owner's bandwidth, not the market.
How Vendor OS solves it — flow from the vendor's perspective
Why vendors will pay for this
A 5-truck operator gets their first WhatsApp enquiry via Vendor OS booking link. They quote in 60 seconds — the system builds the PDF. The client approves with one tap. The driver gets a WhatsApp link, starts GPS. The invoice auto-generates on delivery. The owner sees AED 2,400 earned this week in their dashboard. It took them 3 minutes of total effort. That experience — once — is all the pitch they need.

● Phase 3 — Month 6+
We start with phone-based GPS from day one — it's free and works for 80% of use cases. Once we have a vendor base from the marketplace and Vendor OS, we introduce hardware GPS devices (Teltonika FMB920) on vendor trucks at AED 80–150/device/month. The hardware pays back in under 3 months. Once the device is on the truck, the vendor cannot leave — it is physically locked to our platform. That is the switching cost that builds our moat.
Teltonika FMB920 Phone GPS → Hardware GPS AED 80–150/device/mo <3 month payback
The Problem — why phone GPS alone isn't enough
🔋
Phone GPS dies mid-trip
Driver's battery hits 5%. Screen turns off. GPS stops. Client's tracking map goes blank. The vendor has no idea where their asset is. This happens on every third job.
🚜
Equipment has no phone
A crane, generator, or compressor rented for 3 months on a construction site doesn't have a phone attached to it. Phone GPS works for trucks. It cannot work for equipment assets.
🏗️
Enterprise needs hardware
A main contractor managing AED 50M of equipment across 12 sites will not accept "trust the driver's phone." They need a device. Hardware GPS is the threshold for enterprise contracts.
⛽
No fuel or engine data
Phone GPS gives coordinates. Nothing else. Vendors have no idea about fuel consumption, engine hours, or idling time. They cannot predict maintenance. Breakdowns cost AED 30–80K per incident.
💸
Existing hardware is too expensive
Fleet management systems in the UAE cost AED 3,000–8,000/vehicle upfront plus high monthly fees. A 3-truck operator cannot afford this. Nobody has made GPS affordable for SME fleets.
🔓
No switching cost in SaaS alone
A Vendor OS subscriber can cancel and switch to a competitor. A vendor with a Kasper GPS device on every truck cannot — the device is physically on their asset and locked to our platform.

PRODUCT 04
Telematics & Data Intelligence
● Phase 4 — Month 12+
Once GPS hardware is on hundreds of trucks and thousands of jobs are in the database, the data itself becomes the product. Every job, every GPS ping, every ePOD signature adds to a dataset that no competitor can replicate without running the same jobs. Predictive maintenance, AI dispatch, emissions reporting, lane rate indices, AI dashcam scoring, and dynamic pricing — each is a separately sellable product built on data Kasper already owns.
Predictive maintenance AI dispatch ESG / emissions Lane rate index AED 1,500–5,000/month SaaS
The Problem — what UAE fleet operators cannot do today
🔧
Breakdowns are surprises
Vendors have no early warning system. A truck breaks down mid-job. Emergency repair costs AED 30–80K. Client job delayed. Contract penalty. All of this was predictable — with engine hour data.
🚛
Deadhead trips waste 40% of capacity
A truck delivers to Fujairah and drives back empty. 40% of UAE construction fleet kilometres are dead runs. AI dispatch can match a return load in real time and convert that dead run into revenue.
🌿
ESG reporting is impossible
UAE sustainability mandates and KSA Vision 2030 require CO2 per equipment type per hour. Enterprise clients need this for regulatory compliance. Nobody can provide it today without telematics data.
📊
Nobody knows freight rates
There is no published UAE freight rate index by lane. Rates are quoted by feel. Freight forwarders, insurers, and developers have no benchmark. Kasper will own this data after 10,000 jobs.
🎥
Insurance is overpriced
Logistics SMEs pay full fleet insurance rates with no proof of driver behaviour. AI dashcam scoring with incident detection can cut premiums 10–20%. Insurance companies will fund the hardware to get the data.
⚡
Surge demand is invisible
During the Hormuz crisis, demand for land freight surged 733%. Rates didn't adjust in real time. With dynamic pricing on a live supply-demand signal, Kasper captures that upside systematically.
